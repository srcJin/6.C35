Design-Research Question Statement

Generational Echoes: Tracing the Dynamics of Birth and Mortality in America, 1900-2000

Introduction

The 20th century was a period of profound transformation for the United States, marked by significant population growth influenced by both internal developments and global events. The provided dataset gave us the starting and the ending point. However, the history is continuous. Is there a way that we can use the visualization to express such process?

In this visualization, I want to show the dramatic change of US population under the impacts of major events happened during the 20th century. With the provided population change with 100 years of gap, I was curious about their middle stage, what causes the changes of the population and what reasons are deeply rooted with the time.

However, seldom people can live more than 100 years old, meaning these two groups of people has few direct connection. To answer my question, we have to introduce two additional data:

    - the middle state: the population state in 1950s - the mortality statistics: provide the mortality information with reasons to death

The 1900s

The 1900s marked the beginning of United States's unprecedented changes. From a primarily agrarian society to an industrial powerhouse, and the invention of automobility, triggered significant urbanization and industrialization. It marked the beginning of the Progressive Movement. Citizens started to be aware of the social problems of poverty, labor rights, and women's suffrage, which triggered significant strikes and the rise of labor unions.

The 1950s

The 1950s serves as a pivotal point for our visualization. Marked by the aftermath of World War II, the beginning of the Cold War, and significant social and economic transformations, this era experienced the baby boom, fast urbanization, and migration patterns that significantly influenced the population's growth and distribution of the United States

The 2000s

The end of 20 century, while also the beginning of the 21st century, was marked by profound technological advancements, globalization, and significant social changes that continued to reshape the population landscape of the United States. This era shows the maturation of the baby boom generation and the advanced medical achievements that significantly lowered the death rate.

Design Rationale for Sketch 1:

  • Express Relationships: Using the style of Sanky diagrams, this sketch aims to show the three interconnected generation of population and their relationships, in a simplified way.
  • Efficiency and simplicity: It is effective at depicting the flow of populations or resources between different states or groups. The division between the age group is clear.
  • High Adaptbility: Efficient layout offers plenty of rooms to mark key historical events, explainations. It's also effective to accomodate additional data for future changes.

Design Rationale for Sketch 2:

  • Instead of directly showing the data as statistical result, this sketch targeted at using narrative cartoon to show the change of people's lifestyle and living environment through dipictable figures: the change in dwelling styles, the break of gender discrimentation, the adaptation of car transportation, and the raise of high density cities. These changes indirectly reflects the actual amount of data, but directly answers the qustion we proposed for this visualization.
  • 卡通化、故事化的方式让观众更容易接受，有兴趣细致阅读下去。这种图式的成功需要多层的故事线，并且在不同的尺度使用大量的细节做支撑，一旦成功，它将最大化地扩大受众群体，并将思想更直观、广泛地传播出去。
  • The drawback of this approach is the format of cartoon 让精确的数据变得不容易阅读。它更像是一幅画，而不是对数据的展示。这种先入为主的偏见可能让观众忽视掉他背后相关联的数据精确性。尽管我们可以从设计上弱化这类问题，如限制表达的自由度，提供类似chart的形式融入整张图中。

Design Rationale for Sketch 3:

  • Gamified visualization: this sketch represents the flow of population as an arcade game in three stages. 相对于直接表达数据的bar, 这样的设计采用了比喻的手法，通过弹珠机的形式表达出历史的随机性，以及在这其中反复无常，无法预测的人生结局。这颇为讽刺，也让观众在阅读的过程中对过程更感兴趣。
  • 相比上一张图来说，这张可视化的设计更加抽象，可能需要更多的时间才能真正理解其中的意思，但也正因如此，让观众产生了主动探索的兴趣。不同的篮子代表不同的历史事件，而这些事件又与图形两侧的引出说明相关，丰富了信息的层次。
  • 这张图设计的缺点是不可避免地比例过长，在横向屏幕和小屏幕上容易显示不全，或文字太小。
  • 这种设计提供了动态展示的可能性，我们完全可以将其做成一款网页小游戏，编写一些碰撞和重力物理规则，驱动这些小球的运动，最终展示一个动态变化的美国20世纪人口变迁过程。

Reflection

在这个作业中我努力在寻找一种有趣创新的方式去可视化人口统计和其相关的历史事件。从抽象(sketch 1)，具象(sketch 2) 到游戏化的半抽象(sketch 3)，我尝试了可视化这一百年间的历史进程的三种方式。在我看来，他们没有优劣之分，只是需要根据目标人群去判定我们的评价标准。 在下面的Final Design中，我选择sketch 3作为继续发展的对象。首先，通过一定的抽象化，该可视化方式做到了工作量，表达精度和趣味性的平衡。其次，它为未来的动态可视化或游戏化可视化阶段做了准备，能够在不更改原设计的情况下进行动画化。最后，我认为它的拓展性较强，除了1900，1950,2000这三个节点，我们可以在原设计的基础上增加更多年份，就像给游戏增加更多关卡一样，让可视化的趣味性更强。
